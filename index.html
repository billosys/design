Design and Style Guide

Design is a passion for us, here at Billow.

We are deep into the engineering of distributed systems. Yet this is not a myopic focus for us; our approach is holistic. We accomplish our tasks and succeed in our projects by focusing on the details -- but we never loose sight of the big picture ... of how everything is inter-related.

The desired result affects the planning process. Our sense of sense of goodness in architecture and design affect implementation. The journey and the goal cannot be easily separated, and it is this understanding of systems that allows us to recognize the importance of design at every level.

As such, we wanted a place where we could share what we do with design and the aesthetics of our engineers -- both the class of things that we use in common with other communities and organizations, as well as the class of things that we do differently.

For now, most of what we have provided in these pages relates to our visual design standards and grass-roots marketing. In the future, though, will be be updating this content with more information on our engineering design processes and best practices for the systems we build and maintain at Billo.

While this reduced operations overhead, development times are still high: engineering and product teams either need highly-customized versions of AWS services, or need services that are not in the AWS suite.

This is where BilloSystems comes in.
